 I worked out what I think the odds are, what do I do now? Is your estimation of the odds the same as the odds here? If so, you probably want to skip betting on this game.
Otherwise, you probably want to bet on the team who you think are undervalued. That is, if the odds are 70-30, and you think the odds should be 60-40, you want to bet on the underdog. How much do I bet? That's up to you, really. Generally speaking you want to bet less on underdogs than overdogs.
If you think the odds are really messed up in your favour (If a match that's 90-10 on CSGL that you think is 50-50, for instance), then you might want to place a larger amount.
Don't go all in, or even close to it. The key to profiting off CSGO betting is betting consistently - consistently make a profit in small amounts over time, and your inventory will grow.
Of course, you're not betting anything real on this website, but still. Play it like you were for the best results.
Oh, also. You can't bet less than $0.04, or more than $240. It's how CSGL works. How much will I win if I win? Divide the percentage odds for the opposing team by the percentage odds for your team. You'll be paid that amount if your team wins. (So, betting on the underdog in a 60-40 game will get you 60/40 = 1.5 in payoff.
Plus your bet is returned if you win.
Can I cancel/swap my bet? Swap teams, yes. Cancel no.
This is how CSGL does it, and so shall we. Does ECCSGL do overpay/underpay? Overpay no, underpay yes.
For those not aware of CSGL's idiosyncracies - because on CSGL you bet items and not credits or money, there's some curiousities with how payouts happen.
For those betting the max amount ($240), they often find that they get more than they would expect.
Similarly, those expecting small payoffs (generally below $1) may find they get a very limited amount or nothing. ECCSGL mimics this, crudely. To not fall foul to this, make sure you bet more than cents on overdogs. {% endblock %}
